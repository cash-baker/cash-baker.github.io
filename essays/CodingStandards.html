Folks, What's The Deal with Standards?

09 Feb 2023

Sunsetting JSFiddle (Finally)

As the difficulty of the class grew steadily for me, I finally realized how much I loathe JSFiddle. The sidebar takes up practically half the page, its console implementation made testing code a hassle, and UI elements were buggy. Overall, its a serviceable IDE, at best, when it’s not trying to get in your way. When we eventually made the switch to IntelliJ, I only grew to love it more with time. In comparison, the UI can be customized extensively and the application is nowhere near as buggy. It’s not the best IDE on the market, but at the very least, it’s reliable.

Don’t forget to dot your i’s and insert your newLines!

I may be in the minority, but having ESLint supplement IntelliJ makes my coding experience significantly more valuable and maybe even satisfying. I enjoy knowing exactly where and what my mistakes are because of how errors are handled. Most errors have a label, which corresponds with a web page to teach you about a specific standard. It can also fix errors in many cases, but I usually still choose to hand fix them. After a few hours, I quickly got a good feel for abiding by the ESLint standards. It’s gotten to a point where I can recognize what error I have seconds after I see redlines underneath my lines of code. And you know what.. I do enjoy that green check. I live and die by the green check, which is a testament to how useful it is!

Hopes for the future…

There are things that were, and still maintain to be, a struggle for me to fully grasp. I’m still mystified by Underscore and I’m still pounding my head against ES6 concepts. During the stressful nights of attempting to wrap my head around it all, ESLint and IntelliJ helped pick me back up. When writing out a underscore function, for instance, you’re greeted with the function syntax and parameter definitions, which were especially helpful to me. I’m far from the best programmer (as I recently discovered from DNF’ing all my WODs so far) but I’m confidant that my current tool set will aid greatly in raising my own personal standards.
